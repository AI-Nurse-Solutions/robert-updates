My Plan to Retire by June 2027

Some of you have watched me work night shifts in the ICU, come home, and keep building until 3 AM. You've asked: "Rob, when do you sleep?" The honest answer is: not enough. But there's a plan, and it has a date.

By June 2027, I plan to retire from bedside nursing — not because I don't love it, but because I've found a bigger way to serve. The same instinct that made me a good ICU nurse — watching, protecting, catching the thing before it becomes the thing — is what the healthcare AI movement needs most. Nurses aren't end-users of AI. We're the stewards. And right now, no one is building the infrastructure for that role to exist at scale.

That's what I'm building. Two companies. Two books. One operating system for nurse-led AI governance. And a community of nurses who believe AI should serve dignity, not replace it.

Nurse AI OS — Press Release

FOR IMMEDIATE RELEASE

Nurse AI OS Launches This July: The First Operating System Built for Nurse-Led AI Governance

July 2026 — Global

The NAIO Institute today announced the launch of Nurse AI OS (nurse-ai-os.org), a governed, no-PHI platform where nurses learn to steward artificial intelligence without handing over their clinical judgment.

Nurse AI OS is the first operating system of its kind — built not for technologists, but for the nurses who stand between AI tools and the patients they serve. It provides a structured environment where nurses develop AI literacy, practice governance, and prepare for the emerging role of Nurse AI Orchestrator: the institutional authority responsible for safe, ethical AI deployment at the clinical front line.

The platform is built on three pillars:

EDENA Governance Framework — A five-lens stewardship model (Equity & Ethics, Dignity & Data, Environment & Externalities, Nursing Relevance & Nurse Wellbeing, Agency & Action) with tiered autonomy levels from informational support to supervised action. Every AI agent on the platform carries an EDENA governance card that defines its scope, boundaries, and escalation rules.

Florence-X Orchestration — A multi-agent architecture that lets nurses coordinate AI tools the way they coordinate care: with handoffs, escalations, and human gates. Agents propose. Humans judge. Nurses steward.

Nurse Intelligence Network (NIN) — A community layer where nurses learn together, share governance patterns, and build the professional identity of the AI-era nurse. NIN membership includes access to cohorts, governance tools, and the public NAIO Agent Registry of vetted, EDENA-carded AI agents.

"We built Nurse AI OS because the question was never whether AI would reach the bedside — it's already there. The question is whether nurses will have authority over how it's deployed, or whether we'll be treated as end-users of systems we never approved. Nurse AI OS gives nurses the infrastructure to be stewards, not spectators. That's not a nice-to-have. It's a patient safety imperative." — Robert Domondon, RN, CCRN, CSC, CMC, MD | Founder, NAIO Institute

Nurse AI OS launches with a Founding Steward Cohort — a curated group of nurses, nurse leaders, and clinical informaticists who will be the first to complete the NAIO governance curriculum and shape the platform's evolution. Applications are open now at nurse-ai-os.org.

The platform is strictly non-clinical and contains no PHI. It does not make clinical decisions, replace nursing judgment, or constitute medical advice. It is a governance and education environment — the control plane through which nurses develop the authority to oversee AI in their institutions.

People ask how a solo founder runs two companies while still working ICU shifts. The answer: I don't run them alone. I run them with an AI operating system that has the heart of a nurse — governed by EDENA tiers, orchestrated through Florence-X, and accountable to human judgment at every gate.

What AI agents power:

  • Agent Registry vetting and EDENA governance card generation
  • Course and curriculum development pipelines
  • Technology assessment and KB curation
  • Governance documentation and policy drafting

How It Works: The Agent Stack

I operate through a personal AI Chief of Staff — Hermes, running under the NAIO governance layer. Every agent operates within EDENA tiers (Green for safe, informational work; Yellow for supervised tasks; Orange and Red require explicit human approval). Agents propose actions. I judge them. The system stewards execution. No PHI enters the system. No clinical decisions are delegated. Every meaningful action passes through a human gate.

This is the model I'm teaching other nurses to build for themselves. Not because every nurse should become a developer — but because every nurse should have a governed AI system that works for them, amplifies their judgment, and never, ever replaces it.

One founder. Two companies. A dozen AI agents. One human accountable for every decision. That's the point.
